Mona lisa

The *Mona Lisa*, painted by Leonardo da Vinci in the early 16th century, is one of the most famous portraits in the world. Known for her enigmatic smile and captivating gaze, the painting captures a woman, believed to be Lisa Gherardini, with a background of misty, mountainous landscape. Da Vinci's use of soft shading, or *sfumato*, creates a lifelike quality, giving her face a sense of depth and mystery. The *Mona Lisa* is celebrated for its advanced techniques and realism, influencing countless artists and becoming a symbol of Renaissance art. Today, it resides in the Louvre Museum in Paris, drawing millions of visitors each year.
