Founded in 1636, Harvard University is the oldest higher education institution in the United States. Originally called “New College,” it was renamed in 1639 after Reverend John Harvard, who donated half his estate and library to the school. Harvard's mission is to educate leaders and advance knowledge for the betterment of society. Guided by its motto, "Veritas" (Truth), it values inclusivity, intellectual curiosity, and integrity. Located in Cambridge, Massachusetts, Harvard has a storied history of innovation and leadership, producing U.S. presidents, global leaders, and pioneers in various fields.
