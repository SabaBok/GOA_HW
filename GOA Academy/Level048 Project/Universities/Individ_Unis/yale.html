Founded in 1701, Yale University in New Haven, Connecticut, is one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in the United States. Originally established to train clergy, it has since become a global leader in education, research, and the arts. Yale is known for its rigorous academic programs, its commitment to intellectual freedom, and its diverse and inclusive campus community. The university is made up of 14 schools and offers a broad range of undergraduate and graduate programs across various disciplines. Its motto, "Lux et Veritas" (Light and Truth), reflects its dedication to knowledge and enlightenment.
